About the Candidate

Three decades inside the multilateral system.

Dr. Khalilur Rahman brings a rare combination of diplomatic experience, secretariat leadership, and ministerial responsibility — shaped by service to one of the largest troop-contributing nations to UN peacekeeping and a steadfast voice for the Global South.

A candidate from the Asia-Pacific Group, Dr. Rahman has actively participated in sixteen sessions of the General Assembly — engaging directly in its negotiations, high-level meetings, and follow-up processes — and has served in ministerial-level positions in the Government of Bangladesh since 2024.

His career spans both sides of the multilateral table: as a representative of Bangladesh to the General Assembly, and as a senior official inside the United Nations Secretariat in New York and at UNCTAD in Geneva, where he led the Technology Division and chaired the Technology Bank High-Level Panel.

Throughout, he has worked extensively on the development priorities of Least Developed Countries, Land-Locked Developing Countries, and Small Island Developing States — drawing on Bangladesh's institutional memory of speaking for and coordinating LDC positions across multiple UN forums for more than five decades.

The Vision · Six Pillars

An interlocking agenda for the 81st session.

The vision is organized around six interlocking pillars — anchored in the UN Charter, informed by Bangladesh's long record of service to multilateralism, and oriented toward the milestone deliberations of the 81st session.

I
Peace, Security & Justice

Silence the Guns, Amplify the Voices

Prevention and political solutions, protection of civilians, sustaining peace, the Women, Peace & Security agenda, and confronting transnational organized crime — drawing on Bangladesh's legacy as a lead troop-contributing country.

Read pillar I
II
Sustainable Development

No One Left Behind, No Country Left Out

The course-correction toward SDG Summit 2027 — focused on LDCs, LLDCs and SIDS, financing reform from FfD4 onwards, and a stronger interface between the 2030 Agenda and the African Union's Agenda 2063.

Read pillar II
III
Climate & Environment

Our Planet, Our Pact

Delivering on Paris and COP 30, fully operationalizing the Loss & Damage Fund, universal early-warning systems, and bringing climate mobility into the High-Level Meeting on Sea-Level Rise.

Read pillar III
IV
Human Rights & Humanitarian

Rights & Protection — Freedom from Fear and Want

Strengthening the human rights architecture; sustaining humanitarian space and access; protecting refugees and migrants — informed by Bangladesh's stewardship of 1.3 million Rohingya. Marking 25 years of Durban and 40 years of the Right to Development.

Read pillar IV
V
Digital, AI & Next Generation

Innovation with Inclusion

Inclusive implementation of the Global Digital Compact, an Assembly-anchored Global Dialogue on AI Governance, bridging the digital divide, and decent youth employment leveraging emerging technologies.

Read pillar V
VI
Multilateralism & UN80

We the Peoples — Reimagined

Making the General Assembly the engine of member-driven reform under the UN80 Initiative — with transparency, impartiality, gender parity, equitable geographic distribution, and tighter coordination with the Security Council and ECOSOC.

Read pillar VI
